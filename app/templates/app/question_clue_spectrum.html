Clue Giving

Wavelength is all about wildly creative clues. Firm Rules 1. A clue shouldn't combine multiple ideas or give a lot of explanatory context. 2. The clue must exist as a thing in the universe outside of you giving the clue. 3. You aren't allowed to use one side as a double meaning, e.g. "hot" meaning attractive-though 4. You can't use numbers, percentages, ratios, or anything numeric to sneakily suggest the location of the target. Suggestions: 1. Be concise: Try to use five words or fewer 2. No modifiers: Words like "but", "very", "almost", "slightly", and "kind of" nearly always makes a team weigh the thing itself versus the qualifier used 3. Use proper nouns
